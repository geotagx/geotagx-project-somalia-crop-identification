Can you see the method of cultivation?

How do I answer this question? Get some help

This helps us know what sort of machinery and/or tools are used in agricultural activities in an area. Machinery or tools could be damaged during a disaster and will need to be replaced before people can begin working their land again.

Knowing the types of tools people are using can also give an idea of the agricultural production capabilities in an area. Those with with access to machinery and larger fields are likely to produce more than farmers working manually in smaller plots. At the same time, smaller subsistence farms might be more sensitive to drought and reduced yields are very likely to impact the local populations

  • Manual cultivation: All the work is done by hand, or using hand-held, non mechanised tools such as scythes, hand-pushed plows etc. A person working in their fields with their hands would be an example of manual cultivation. However a person standing in the field does not indicate manual cultivation.
  • With animals: Similar to manual cultivation, however animals are used to help. If animals are involved they will often be hooked up to farming equipment
  • By machine: Machines are used to improve the efficiency of cultivation. Look for mechanised equipment and tools such as tractors

How can I identify a crop species?

Identifying the species of crop you are looking at from just a photo can be very difficult. Here are a few tips to help your search:

  • Look at the photo context, often the crop is written in the caption or other associated text.
  • Compare the photos of the examples provided with the photo you are looking at
  • Use the resources available on the internet to help you.
  • Use Google! Follow the link under the thumbnail to begin searching

None of the suggestions match? Try looking for lists of agricultural products for the region or country you are working on. Under the "Other" box there should be a link to a document on the relevant agricultural sector, you can start your search from here.

What to search for?

  • Keywords: "agriculture", "crops", the name of the country in which you are working
  • "What do they grow in XX country?"
  • Agricultural department (or similar), NGO's, or organisations like the Food and Agriculture Organisation (FAO). These organisations often have inventories or reports on the agricultural activities within a particular country.

Identifying a plant can often take some time, so don't get discouraged if you feel it is taking too long! And if you are stuck you can always click the "Unknown" button and move onto the next question.

Are the crops green?

How do I answer this question? Get some help

This gives an indication of if the crops are alive or not. Crops that are not green may have suffered from water stress (What is water stress). If the crops are not green it does not necessarily mean that they are suffering from water stress. Some crops might look dry and dead but in fact they are senescing or drying down at the end of their life-cycle, just before being harvested. This is a normal part of the production cycle for crops such as corn. In this question, however, we just need to know if the crops are green or not.

Click Yes if you can see green in the plant

Click No if the plants have no green at all, or less than 10% green

Do the plants seem to be wilting? Or their leaves rolling?

How do I answer this question? Get some help

Plants take water up from the soil. The water passes through the plant and evaporates via small pores called stomata on the leaves. This process is called transpiration.

Rolling leaves in plants is a defence mechanism to minimise transpiration during times of water stress so the plant won’t lose too much water (Click here for more info). Therefore rolling leaves are a sign that there might be drought.

Wilting of plants indicates that cells have begun to die and break down because of lack of water. Wilting plants might still be green but look limp, like they are falling over. They may even have begun to lose their green colour. The plant loses its green colour when the cell dies and the chlorophyll in the plant breaks down.

Does the crop appear to have been harvested?

How do I answer this question? Get some help

If the crop has been successfully harvested prior to or during the disaster then there was likely some food from it.

Signs of harvest could be:

  • Plants that are bent over or cut
  • Piles of plants that were cut down
  • Plants that look like the tops were cut off

Are there any plants missing from the rows?

How do I answer this question? Get some help

When crops are planted, seeds are typically spaced a set distance apart. Missing plants could be the result of soil conditions that are too dry for germination, or the seed germinated but the soil was too dry for the seedling.

Look at the rows of crops, if the gap between two plants looks unusually large relative to the average gap in the row, there are probably plants missing. In the photo below, note the larger gaps between some of the plants in the foreground. You should select "Yes" in these cases
