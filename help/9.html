When crops are planted, seeds are typically spaced a set distance apart. Missing plants could be the result of soil conditions that are too dry for germination, or the seed germinated but the soil was too dry for the seedling.

Look at the rows of crops, if the gap between two plants looks unusually large relative to the average gap in the row, there are probably plants missing. In the photo below, note the larger gaps between some of the plants in the foreground. You should select in these cases
